Shakib Kafashan

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Transportation Systems Engineering at the University of California, Irvine (UCI). My research focuses on simulation-based safety assessment of mixed human-driven and autonomous vehicle traffic, with an emphasis on cyber-physical resilience, intelligent control, and data-driven risk prediction.

More broadly, I work at the intersection of machine learning, reinforcement learning, optimization, and traffic simulation. I build end-to-end ML workflows, surrogate models, and decision-support tools for transportation safety, network design, and vehicle electrification.

Before joining UCI, I received my B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from Sharif University of Technology in 2022.

Publications

2025
Leveraging Wide and Deep Learning for Urban Bus Accident Severity Prediction
Shakib Kafashan, Fatemeh Kafashan, R. Jayakrishnan, Amir Hajbabaie
Transportation Research Board (TRB), 2025 (accepted)

We develop a Wide & Deep neural network for predicting injury and fatality risk in urban bus crashes. The model uses ADASYN for imbalance handling and calibrated decision thresholds, significantly outperforming traditional statistical and tree-based baselines while surfacing interpretable risk factors via SHAP.

Red Teaming the Road: End-to-End Cyber Attacks to Closed-Loop Resilience in CAV Networks
Shakib Kafashan, R. Jayakrishnan
Reliability Engineering & System Safety, 2025 (under preparation)

We construct a mixed HV/AV traffic simulation with intelligent controllers and model cyber-physical attacks such as false-data injection and sensor loss. Surrogate safety models and reinforcement learning are used to quantify attack impact and design resilient control policies.

Solving the DNDP with Binary Detours: Outer Approximation, Value-Function Cuts, and Column Generation
Shaghayegh Nouhi, Shakib Kafashan, Michael Levin
Transportation Research Part C, 2025 (under preparation)

We propose an exact branch-and-price-and-cut framework for the detour signage network design problem (DNDP), combining outer approximation, value-function cuts, and column generation. The method delivers globally optimal detour sets with substantial reductions in computational time on benchmark networks such as Sioux Falls and Anaheim.

2024
Electrification of Off-Road Vehicles — A Comparative Economic Analysis of Electric and Diesel Machinery
Shakib Kafashan, Jean-Daniel Saphores
Transportation Research Board (TRB), 2024 (accepted)

We conduct a total cost of ownership analysis for electric versus diesel construction equipment, incorporating Monte Carlo simulation, social cost of carbon, and future price scenarios. The study identifies conditions under which zero-emission machinery becomes economically competitive and discusses policy levers to accelerate adoption.

Designed an end-to-end crash-severity classification pipeline with feature engineering, ADASYN imbalance handling, and threshold calibration.
